## 2. Brand architecture (finalized, in scope)

Foodbiz operates as a parent brand with **five** specialized sub-brands. The parent does the full spread of catering work; the sub-brands sharpen the offering for specific segments. The architecture below reflects the founders' direction as of 20 May 2026 — Moira replaces the older "Foodbiz Weddings" name, and Pillar Live joins as the protocol-events sub-brand alongside Caterpillar (which keeps its recurring-corporate focus).

### 2.1 Foodbiz (parent)
- **What it does**: The master brand. Continues to cover weddings, corporates, and events for clients who come in through the top of the funnel without a specific sub-brand pull.
- **Visual identity**: Teal wordmark with spoon-and-fork-in-plate icon. Tagline strip: "weddings • corporates • events". Refresh status: TBD.
- **Tone today**: Energetic, accomplished, slightly cheeky in the older deck ("This is how we groove it"). The new brand-voice direction will inherit some of that warmth but elevate it (see §7).

### 2.2 Moira (sub-brand — wedding specialty)
- **What it does**: Wedding hospitality. Multi-day weddings, ceremony-by-ceremony culinary planning, cross-cultural / cross-cuisine menu design, full wedding-event production.
- **Naming note**: **Moira replaces "Foodbiz Weddings"**. Foodbiz Weddings is being retired as a customer-facing name. The legacy gold/glitter "Foodbiz Weddings" wordmark in `logos/` is reference-only — keep on file, but it's no longer the wedding mark.
- **Visual identity**: Logo to be designed. Palette inherits from the retired Foodbiz Weddings mark — gold or copper, per the 23 May 2025 meeting log.
- **Strategy note**: The Content Bank's "MOIRA" section (~lines 521–608 of `Foodbiz_ Content Bank.md`) captures the strategic positioning — *sacred unions, deep cultural intelligence, emotional curation* — but the language register itself has been rejected by the founders. Use it for strategy, not for copy.

### 2.3 Caterpillar (sub-brand — recurring corporate)
- **What it does**: Recurring and sustained corporate catering — executive dining programs, office cafés, ongoing institutional catering, day-to-day corporate execution.
- **Visual identity**: Bold black "Cater Pillar" wordmark with a stacked-bowl caterpillar mark in red/yellow/green.
- **Strategy note**: Stays in its lane — does NOT cover flagship/protocol events. Those sit with Pillar Live.

### 2.4 Pillar Live (sub-brand — high-stakes / protocol events)
- **What it does**: Large, public, high-stakes events. G20-style summits, defence and government events, large brand-led public spectacles, civic/national programmes, large-scale launches with protocol requirements. Engineered for complexity, precision, and visibility.
- **Visual identity**: Logo to be designed. Visual direction TBD.
- **Strategy note**: The Content Bank's "PILLAR LIVE" section (~lines 789–882) captures the positioning — *high-stakes protocol execution, large-scale complexity management* — useful for strategy and audience definition.

### 2.5 Gummy Gourmet (sub-brand, newest — children's catering)
- **What it does**: Gourmet catering for children. Positioned by the founders as a "one of a kind catering company for children".
- **Visual identity**: Playful, colorful, bear-face logo with a spoon as the nose. Tone is dramatically lighter than the other brands.
- **Strategic relevance**: Newest initiative — likely the most differentiated and most marketable angle vs. competitors, who don't have a dedicated children's vertical at this quality level.

### 2.6 Concept explored but NOT in scope
Surfaced in prior strategy work; do not surface to the client unless they reopen it: **The Traveling Cirque / Circ** — proposed artisanal gourmet product line.

## 3. What they actually do — service mix

Drawn from the 2025 presentation and Content Bank. Foodbiz frames its work across many event archetypes:

- **Corporate & business**: launches, summits, exhibitions, AGMs, conferences, internal events, executive dining
- **Brand activations**: fashion shows, luxury retail launches, automotive launches, sports events, music festivals
- **Weddings**: traditional, contemporary, cross-cultural, destination
- **Children's events**: birthdays and family celebrations (now under Gummy Gourmet)
- **Government / institutional**: high-profile civic and defence events
- **Social-media-led activations**: creator launches, Pop-ups
- **Curated experiential**: themed dinners, signature fusions, regional showcases

Range of cuisines they market: global cuisines, regional ethnic Indian, traditional, contemporary, fusion, "grandma's kitchen" nostalgia, signature fusion.

## 5. Values, principles, and language they're attached to

From the Content Bank and the 23 May 2025 meeting log:

- **Hassle-free** — central value. "When you work with us, outsource all stress to us so you can be present and enjoy."
- **Service and experience as the highest priority** — repeatedly stated by the founders.

- **Transparency and honesty** — surfaced as a stated brand value in the 2025 deck.
- **Indian culinary heritage with modern execution** — strong theme in all strategic content.

## 6. Visual / design direction (per founders, 23 May 2025)

What they like:
- Contemporary / modern aesthetic, "international, like out of Denmark"
- Typography-led or monogram-led marks (or text-with-an-element)
- Gold + pastel palette
- Colors lifted from 50s/70s luxury car palettes
- Wedding mark in gold or copper
- Indigo was a logo color all founders liked

What they don't want:
- Orange or green (especially in the corporate identity)
- Generic catering tropes (chef hats, crossed cutlery clichés, etc.)

Asset surfaces to keep in mind: placards, aprons, table cards, menus, signage, vehicle livery, social tiles, deck templates.

## 7. Voice direction (settled — voice doc to be produced)

The founders have **rejected the language in the Content Bank** — too technical / over-engineered, and uses words "like a typical catering brand does." That includes the ultra-luxury-philosophical register (*intentionality, reverence, art of offering, anticipatory grace, unseen choreography, sacred union*) and the generic-catering-cliché register both.

The right direction borrows from:
- **The 2021/2025 deck voice** — punchy, energetic, accessible. "This is how we groove it", "Where excellence meets elegance".
- **The founders' own stated values** — *hassle-free*, *food + experience*, transparency, service-as-priority.
- **The 23 May 2025 visual brief** — international, contemporary, Denmark-clean — language should feel modern and confident, not heavy or ornamental.

Per-sub-brand modulations to keep in mind when the voice doc is drafted:
- **Foodbiz parent** — assured, range-confident, slightly cheeky, accessible.
- **Moira** — warm, intimate, present-tense, ceremony-aware. Avoid "sacred / sanctified / reverence" theatrics.
- **Caterpillar** — practical, dependable, considered. Not cold. Not corporate-jargon.
- **Pillar Live** — composed, precise, large-frame. Conveys capability without grandstanding.
- **Gummy Gourmet** — playful, parent-friendly, food-positive for kids. Dramatically lighter register.

A separate `brand-voice.md` will codify do/don't vocab, headline patterns, CTA library, and worked examples. That doc must exist and be signed off before any customer-facing copy gets written.

## 8. Competitive landscape (from Drive research doc)

The "Research for Foodbizz Meeting" doc benchmarks Foodbiz against the top premium-catering operators in India and globally. Names to know:

- **India — premium / luxury**: Curated Catering by Design (Mayank Prasad — strong reference, global expansion, Mercedes / Paytm / Google / Tata client list), Foodlink Luxury Catering (funded, destination weddings, Armani Hotel, Lake Como), Sage & Saffron, Saltt Catering, The Purple Plate, Fossetta Gourmet Catering (Amex, Deloitte, ICMR, AIIMS, IIT Delhi, WEF), Moets Catering (Microsoft, Nestlé, Pepsi, Tata, NDTV), The Food Affairs
- **India — institutional**: Compass Group India
- **Global**: CCI Creative Cuisines Inc., Hutten Catering BV, DO & CO Aktiengesellschaft, Elior Group S.A.

The competitive insight the research lands on: premium operators win on **emotional storytelling**, **clear positioning**, and **guest-experience visuals** — not on listing services. Foodbiz's current online presence underplays the names it has actually served.
